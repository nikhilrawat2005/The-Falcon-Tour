Baku's Quiet Comeback: Azerbaijan's Underrated Week

5 min read·6 Day Itinerary·By The Falcon Tour Team
Azerbaijan doesn't get the coverage it should. Baku alone is worth the flight — a Silk Road old town wedged against a skyline of glass flame-shaped towers — and six days is enough to add the mud volcanoes, the mountains and the Caspian coast around it.

Baku: old city, new skyline

Two days here split cleanly: the Old City (İçəri Şəhər) with the Maiden Tower and the Shirvanshahs' Palace on day one, and the Flame Towers plus a Caspian boardwalk walk at sunset on day two — when the towers' LED display switches from flame to flag to waterfall.

The mud volcanoes, genuinely strange

"Gobustan's mud volcanoes are one of the odder day trips we book anywhere — a landscape of small, cold, bubbling cones about 40 minutes outside Baku, alongside ancient rock petroglyphs dating back thousands of years."

Into the mountains: Sheki and Gabala

Two days head inland to Sheki — a former Silk Road stop with a khan's palace of stained-glass windows — and Gabala, a mountain town with cable cars and a noticeably cooler climate than the coast.

  • Sheki's khan's palace: no nails used in its original construction — built entirely from interlocking wood.
  • Gabala cable car: the Tufandag ride gives the best panorama of the Caucasus foothills.
  • Local currency: cards are accepted in cities but carry manat for the smaller towns.

A last Caspian evening

The final day returns to Baku's boulevard for a Caspian Sea sunset and a last plate of plov before the flight out — a short trip that leaves most travellers already planning a return for the parts they didn't have time for.
