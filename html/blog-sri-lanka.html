Sri Lanka Slow Travel: Tea Hills to Turquoise Coast

6 min read·7 Day Itinerary·By The Falcon Tour Team
Sri Lanka packs a disproportionate amount of country into a small island — tea hills, ancient rock fortresses, wildlife parks and a coastline that swings between surf towns and quiet coves. Seven days is enough if the route doesn't backtrack.

Kandy and the hill country

We start inland, not on the coast — Kandy's Temple of the Tooth and the botanical gardens at Peradeniya, then the train up to Ella. The Kandy–Ella railway is one of those routes people plan whole trips around, and for good reason: tea estates rolling past the window for hours, no highway in sight.

Ella: hikes and one iconic bridge

Little Adam's Peak at sunrise for a manageable climb with a big view, the Nine Arch Bridge for the photo everyone's seen, and a tea factory tour to understand what's growing on every hillside around town.

South to Yala and the coast

A morning safari in Yala National Park — leopards aren't guaranteed, but elephants and peacocks essentially are — before continuing to the south coast. Mirissa for whale watching in season, Galle Fort for a walkable colonial old town, and Unawatuna for a beach day that isn't trying to impress anyone.

  • Safari timing: early morning or late afternoon — midday game drives see far less.
  • Galle Fort: stay for sunset on the ramparts, skip the midday heat.
  • Coastal train: the Colombo–Galle line runs right along the water — worth one leg of the trip.

The close: Colombo, briefly

One night in Colombo before the flight out — enough for a Galle Face Green evening and a proper Sri Lankan curry spread, without pretending the capital needs more than that on this itinerary.
