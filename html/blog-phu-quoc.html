Phu Quoc: Vietnam's Island Exhale

4 min read·5 Day Itinerary·By The Falcon Tour Team
Not every trip needs an itinerary. Phu Quoc is the island we point travellers to when the ask is simply 'somewhere quiet, with good food and clear water' — five days with just enough structure to see the island without ever feeling scheduled.

Day one: arrive and do nothing

We deliberately leave day one empty. Check in, walk the beach in front of the hotel, and eat dinner at whichever seafood shack looks busiest with locals rather than tourists — usually the best sign on the island.

The cable car and Sun World

The Hon Thom cable car holds the record for the longest three-rope cable car over the sea — an eight-minute ride with the whole southern coastline visible below, ending at a small theme park and beach worth half a day.

Fish sauce, pearls, and the night market

Phu Quoc's fish sauce factories have made the island's version famous across Vietnam — a quick, genuinely interesting stop. The night market in Duong Dong is where dinner happens most evenings: grilled seafood picked live and cooked to order.

  • Snorkelling: An Thoi archipelago boat trips are the easiest way to see the island's reefs.
  • Sunset side: the west coast beaches face the right way for it — Long Beach or Ong Lang.
  • Pace: resist the urge to fill every day — this is the one stop built for empty afternoons.
